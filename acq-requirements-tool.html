Speed test logic: A requirement passes when it is outcome-defined (not solution-prescribed), performance thresholds are testable within a prototype period, and evaluation criteria do not exclude non-traditional contractors.

Phase 03 · Scale Test
can the solution
scale to demand?
Assess whether the requirement is designed to receive a solution that can be produced, deployed, and sustained at the volume and geography the mission demands.
03
Scale test logic: Requirements that anchor to single-vendor solutions, lack transition-to-production provisions, or omit supply chain considerations routinely fail to scale beyond the prototype unit.

Phase 04 · Operational Capability Test
will it survive
contact with the mission?
Determine whether the requirement is written to produce a capability that operates in the environment — not one that passes a controlled T&E event and fails in the field.
04
Ops capability logic: A requirement that specifies performance only in ideal conditions, omits operator integration, or defers logistics to a future phase produces systems that succeed in testing and fail in the field.
